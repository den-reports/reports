_Researched on 2026-04-21 UTC. I only kept opportunities with a live public CFP path and a deadline that was still open on the research date. I excluded clearly closed CFPs and weak-fit business events._

## Recommended shortlist

If TestSprite only has bandwidth for a few applications this cycle, I would prioritize:

1. **KubeCon + CloudNativeCon North America 2026** - biggest platform-engineering audience and a strong fit for AI testing + developer productivity.
2. **DevTalks Romania 2026** - large CEE developer audience plus dedicated DevOps and Quality Engineering / Automation stages.
3. **SREday NYC Q2 2026** - focused reliability audience that will care about production-safe AI testing.
4. **Conf42 Database DevOps 2026** - narrower but highly relevant audience for schema safety, release confidence, and observability.
5. **DevOpsDays Berlin 2026** - strong practitioner crowd and a good fit for a lessons-learned talk.

## Why these are the best fits for TestSprite

- **Strong audience fit:** I biased toward DevOps, SRE, quality engineering, test automation, and platform engineering audiences that will immediately understand TestSprite's value.
- **Good stage fit for TestSprite's positioning:** The strongest stages are the ones where TestSprite can talk about **AI testing that improves release confidence**, not just testing theory.
- **Mix of reach and efficiency:** This list balances big-brand stages (KubeCon, DevTalks) with smaller, higher-signal events where TestSprite can stand out (SREday, Conf42, city DevOpsDays).

## Notes on research quality

- I intentionally **excluded closed CFPs** and weak-fit generic business events.
- Some conference sites were partially flaky from this worker (especially redirect-heavy DevOpsDays pages), so where that happened I kept the **official event URL** plus the **public CFP URL** that still resolved and showed the active deadline.
- For **ITC 2026**, I included it as the lowest-priority row because the official CFP is paper-oriented rather than a classic practitioner talk submission. The other 14 are better first targets for TestSprite.
